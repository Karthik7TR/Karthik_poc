Part II. Damages Relating to Particular Subject Areas
Chapter 14. Attachment, Writ of Possession, Garnishment, Execution and Exemption
D. Execution
§ 14:22. Redemption of real property

  • Graham and Keller, West's Kentucky Practice, Domestic Relations Law § 17:4
Treatises and Practice Aids
  • Mapother and Canary, Kentucky Collections § 13:14
  • West's Kentucky Practice, Methods of Practice § 39:8
If land which is sold under execution does not bring two-thirds of its appraised value, 1 the defendant may redeem it. By paying the purchaser the price paid plus 10% per year within six months from the day of sale, the defendant may redeem the land. 2 The defendant should then take the purchaser's receipt and file it with the clerk of the court. 3 The defendant should tender the redemption money to the purchaser or his or her agent in the county where the land lies or where the judgment was obtained. If the tender is refused, the defendant should file with the clerk of the court an affidavit concerning the tender and refusal and the redemption money. 4 The defendant can retain possession of land until his or her right of redemption expires. 5
It should be noted that the right of redemption may also be executed upon and sold. The defendant, however, retains a right of redemption against both purchasers. 6
