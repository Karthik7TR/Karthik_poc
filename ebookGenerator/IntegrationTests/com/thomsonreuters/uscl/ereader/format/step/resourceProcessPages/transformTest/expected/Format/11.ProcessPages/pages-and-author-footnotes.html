WHAT’S NEW
2019 Edition
This 2019 edition of the Multidistrict Litigation Manual brings this pathfinding work current on the many types of cases involved in multidistrict litigation. Most of the updated content comes in decisions from the Judicial Panel. The Panel’s approach to transfer decisions evolve every year, and these changes this year include:

  • • Scores of decisions where the Panel has articulated reasons for denying transfer

  • • Analysis of the Panel's increasing preference for use of transfer under 28 U.S.C.A. § 1404(a) for all purposes

  • • A continued tendency of the Panel to explain its transfer decisions in terms of decisions in similar dockets in prior years

  • • Elaboration on the Panel's concerns about creating dockets including litigation against competing business, recognizing how that would increase the complexity of the litigation rather than ease it

  • • Continuing recognition by the Panel that dockets where the same counsel appear in the cases are inherently less complex, and possibly less likely to be centralized

  • • Considerations relating to selection of a transferee district and judge, including experience in similar cases, pendency of current and earlier cases, and even private practice experience of the judge
The Panel continues to consider a wide variety of factors in deciding whether to transfer cases and where to transfer them, and several decisions illustrate the Panel’s willingness to transfer cases to experienced judges who have not presided or are not currently presiding over an MDL docket.
