Part I. Handling Cases Before the Judicial Panel on Multidistrict Litigation
Chapter 4. Procedure Before the Panel
§ 4:7. Initiating Panel proceedings—Method of filing
For attorneys, who are required to be ECF users, filing can be accomplished only by filing electronically. For non-ECF uses, filing a motion for transfer with the Panel can be accomplished only in one way—by mailing it or delivering it to the address required in the Rules:
Clerk of the Panel
Judicial Panel on Multidistrict Litigation
Thurgood Marshall Federal Judiciary Building
One Columbus Circle, N.E., Room G-255, North Lobby
Washington, D.C. 20002-8041
A party filing electronically is required to serve courtesy copy on the Panel within 24 hours of filing. For non-ECF filers, this extra copy is filed simultaneously with the initial filing. The Rule explicitly forbids the delivery or mailing of papers to a single judge of the Panel. This Rule serves the general interest of requiring a single central location for filing documents. It is especially important for the Panel because the judges are normally located in various locations, each far from the Clerk's office, and it would place a significant and unnecessary burden on the judges of the Panel to have to receive filings and forward them to the Clerk and on the Clerk to monitor litigation with transnational filing. It also prevents what might be viewed as ex parte filing with a single Panel member, which could be viewed by a litigant as unfair. By operation of rule, it is improper.
If a party is required to provide paper copies to the Panel, either U.S. Mail or private courier (UPS, FedEx, etc.) may be used. In practice many of the Panel's submissions are received by each available method.
The procedure outlined above is the only method by which a party can invoke the statute to request transfer and consolidation or coordination of pretrial proceedings. A request for transfer that is not filed with the Panel will simply be ignored by the Panel and cannot be acted on by any other judge or court. In one case a party filed a motion for transfer under 28 U.S.C.A. § 1407 with the court in which an action was pending. The court was required to deny the motion without considering its potential merits.
